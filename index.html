Hello!

Thank you for checking out my final project for COMS3430! This is a step sequencer
featuring a sine synthesizer in the treble range, a saw synthesizer in the bass range,
several percussion samples, and optional effects. I have some tips below for fun things
you can do on this page, but I think the best way to approach it is to just start
clicking around and see what the page can do for you. Enjoy!

Motivation

In general, I am most interested in using technology to give musicians platforms for
expressing and expanding their own creativity. This is essentially like inventing new musical
instruments, but digitally. Every musician has preferences and opinions on what sounds "good"
that are shaped by their own influences and experiences. I think of this as something of a
"musical personality," and I think it is important to give musicians as many options and platforms
as possible for expressing their "musical personality." My aim for this step sequencer was to create
a platform that has enough features to let musicians' "personalities" comes through, while
maintaining a somewhat controlled environment so that it would be difficult to create something
altogether horrendous.

Features

Here are the features included in this step sequencer.

Instruments:

-Treble Synth:

A polyphonic sinewave synth with a range from D4 to D5.

-Bass Synth:

A polyphonic sawtooth synth with a range from D2 to D3.

-Percussion Samples:

Sampled percussion hits, including ride, crash, two hats, shaker,
clap, two rims, snare, tom, and kick.

Options:

-AM Synthesis:

Options to toggle AM synthesis for both the treble synth and the
the bass synth. Slider to adjust the AM frequency.

-Low-pass Filter:

Options to add a low-pass filter on both synths and each sample sound.
Sliders to adjust the cutoff frequency for each filter.

-Vibrato:

Option to add vibrato to the treble synth. Sliders to adjust the frequency
and depth of the vibrato.

-Convolution:

Options to convolve the percussion samples with external sound clips. Options
for convolution are:
-Kellyanne Conway saying "Alternative Facts"
-Kamala Harris saying "Mr. Vice President, I'm speaking"
-Donald Trump saying "Radical Left Supporters"

Global-Usage:

-BPM:

Slider to adjust the BPM of the sequencer. New BPM only takes effect after stopping
and restarting the sequencer.

-Mouseover:

When not enabled, notes can be triggered by clicking the grey dots. When a grey dot
turns green, the note is enabled. Mouseover allows the user to toggle notes by hovering
their mouse over the grey dot, making for faster triggering.

-Trail:

Trailing is dependent on a number, the trailFactor, which is initially set to 5 and can
be adjusted in the field next to the "toggle Trail" button. When trail is enabled, if the
number of triggered notes exceeds trailFactor, triggering a new note will simultaneously
disable the note triggered trailFactor notes ago. To see this effect clearly, turn on both
mouseover and trail and run your mouse over the buttons.

Ideas for getting started

Techno

The design of this sequencer is well suited for quickly making a techno-style loop. There
are 16 beats where users can trigger notes. Consider these as 4 principle beats with 16th notes
in between. The default BPM is set to 120 (in terms of the principle beats).

Trigger kick drums on beats 1, 5, 9, and 13. Now trigger a hat on beats 3, 7, 11, and 15.
Instantly, you have a basic techno rhythm to work off of. From here, have fun clicking around
in the synths to create a bass riff and a melody, and mess around with the options to shape the
sound how you like it.

Evolving Rhythms

Enable trail mode and set the trailFactor to 30, and enable one of the convolutions. Start the
sequencer, and start clicking randomly in the percussion sounds. At first it won't sound like much,
but eventually a rhythm will start to form. Once you have enough notes triggered and you start to
see them trailing off as well, slow down and only trigger notes every second or so. What you'll have
is a continually evolving rhythm, where new elements are added and old ones are removed with each
new click.
